Information

Context

This very first edition of the Insect Spatial Awareness and Environmental Manipulation Workshop aims to bring together researchers and students who are interested in how insects perceive, encode and react to their immediate surroundings.

The workshop will deliberately be small scale, to foster meaningful discussions and connections in an informal atmosphere.

Research topics: active sensing, tool use, nest building, object transport, prey catching ...and anything else involving the use of information in the peripersonal space of insects

Talks

Talks should ideally be targeted: Describe what the specific terms mean, what is the current state of the art for your particular research question, what are the open questions, which new tools need to be developed, etc.

  • ~30 minutes long
  • Early-career researchers or students preferred
  • Be interactive, casual, expect questions

Talks may be accompanied by posters (see below).

Posters

Accompanying posters that provide additional context to the talks are greatly encouraged, but optional.

There will be no "poster sessions" per se, but we will rather organise the food & drinks breaks around the posters in order to create a more relaxed atmosphere for discussions.

  • Any usual poster format will do
  • Feel free to bring posters from earlier conferences / workshops if relevant
  • Be interactive, casual, expect questions
